Mechanics:

Newton's second law of motion states that there is a proportion between net force acting on an object and the object's acceleration. The coefficient of proportionality is the objects mass, the equation is as follows:

ΣF=ma


ΣF - the sum of all forces acting on the body
a - the object's acceleration
m - the object's mass

For example: If I'm pushing a body with a mass of 2kg with a force of 10N((read out as 10 Newtons) the body's acceleration would then be 5m/s^2. the ball will start accelerating, it's velocity will increase by 5m/s every second.

From newton's 2nd law we can conclude that if ΣF=0 then a=0 and if a=0 the ΣF=0. this is called Newton's first law of motion. In other words, if all forces acting on a body cancel out then the body doesn't change is velocity and vice versa. For example, if I push a ball with a force of 5N And there is wind blowing in the opposite direction, also applying a force of 5N then the ball will simply not move. But, If the wind were to start blowing before I push the balloon and the wind gives It an initial velocity and only then I start pushing the baloon, the baloon will remain in the same initial velocity. let's plug the values in the equation and confirm it. ΣF =5N-5N=0=ma, therefor a=0 and the velocity doesn't change.

For Newton's third law consider this thought experiment: your hand is pushing a table, thus making the table accelerate. Your hand is also accelerating with the table and they are moving at equal speeds. Relative to the hand, the table is not moving, thus atable=0(Relative to the hand). According to Newton's first law ΣFtable=0. If the hand is applying a force F to the table then there has to be a force of magnitude F and an opposite direction to F such that: ΣFtable=F+(-F)=0.

We conclude from here Newton's third law which states all that when a force is apllied on a body the body will apply an opposite force with the same magnitude.
